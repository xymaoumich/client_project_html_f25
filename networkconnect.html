Networking and Connecting

Building a Strategic Networking Plan

Building a clear, intentional networking plan is an essential part of career development. By creating a LAMP list, you can organize your outreach and focus on high-priority contacts. For a structured approach, see the [Handout] Creating a LAMP List for Strategic Networking.

The Intro to Making Connections Workshop highlights strategies for identifying alumni, crafting effective outreach emails and LinkedIn messages, and sustaining professional relationships. It emphasizes keeping messages concise, personal, and respectful of the recipient’s time. The workshop also provides templates for coffee chat requests, internal referrals, and thank-you notes.

To further connect with alumni, visit the University Career Alumni Networking (UCAN) platform, which allows you to reach out directly to U-M alumni across industries.

The AI Prompt Resource Guide introduces ways to use generative AI responsibly to support networking, from drafting outreach messages to brainstorming conversation starters. It includes prompt styles such as scenario, role-based, and list generation. For example: “Imagine you are at a networking event—how would you introduce yourself?”

A strong elevator pitch is another critical tool for making connections. You can review the [Handout] Elevator Pitch Rubric to refine your self-introduction.

Conference Prep Workshop

Attending conferences can be a powerful way to expand your professional network. The Conference Prep Workshop focuses on setting clear goals, identifying speakers, alumni, or employers you want to meet, and reaching out ahead of time with tailored LinkedIn messages. During the event, dress professionally, carry an updated resume, and practice your elevator pitch.

A helpful planning resource is available here: Conference Preparation & Networking Template .

Outreach Messages

Reaching out effectively is at the heart of networking. For sample outreach language, review the Networking Outreach Email Examples , which provide templates for different situations.

For additional variations, explore Networking Email Templates from Resume Worded , a tool that generates multiple approaches to professional outreach.

To enhance your professional communication, you can also refer to Lettersmith (video overview) and Lettersmith Quick Reference Guide .

Planning an Informational Interview

Informational interviews are informal conversations with professionals that give you insight into industries, organizations, and roles. The TIARA Method offers a structure for your questions: Trends, Insight, Advice, Resources, and Assignments. A typical flow might start with light conversation, move into career-related questions, and end with gratitude and a follow-up thank-you email.

For question examples and further detail, see the [Handout] Informational Interviews - TIARA Method .

Career Fair Preparation

Career fairs provide opportunities to meet employers and practice your pitch in a fast-paced environment. The Career Fair Prep Workshop recommends registering in advance, researching employers, and preparing multiple copies of your resume. Start with lower-priority employers to practice, then approach your top choices.

Following up afterward is critical—send thank-you emails or LinkedIn messages within one to two days. For a walkthrough of what to expect, watch the Career Fair Prep Workshop recording .
